GRA 431 - Interactive Animation for Web

Assignment

To create an interactive animation package consisting of one interactive animation in various formats for use on the internet. Using animation on the web has presented digital artists with the challenge of creating visually appealing and thorough animations while maintaining a file size that allows for optimal functionality. It will be your job to consider file size throughout your interactive animation and employ strategies and techniques that maintain intended functionality. The purpose of your interactive animation is to inform viewers about a topic of your choice. For example, if you are a hunter, you may choose to create your interactive animation about hunting safety techniques. This animation must present this information in a visually appealing way that maintains viewer interest and requires viewer interaction throughout.

Design Process

For my interactive animation project, I developed an infographic aimed at educating users on the proper care of pet betta fish, which are frequently mistreated due to a widespread lack of knowledge about their proper husbandry. I utilized various design elements created in Adobe Illustrator, including images of fish, plants, tanks, and other accessories. To enhance the experience, I added fun tutorial music in the background and designed interactive buttons to give fun facts or highlight specific care tips. The key to making the interactivity effective was to construct a compelling argument that conveyed the importance of the topic, which is a subject I'm passionate about. My aim was to translate this passion into the project. My implementation plan for sound involved having effects trigger when users interact with elements such as moving a plant into the tank or adding substrate. These sounds were programmed to play upon user interaction with the buttons. The project was executed using two main programs: Adobe Illustrator and Animate. In Illustrator, I worked on a simple 8.5x11 artboard in RGB color mode to create the primary design elements. Then, I employed Animate on the HTML5 Canvas platform to bring all the elements together and animate them as intended.

Review

In reviewing the final design, I recognize that while it demonstrates a high level of craftsmanship, there is still room for progress, particularly in mastering the finer aspects of design presentation and animation. The clarity and resolution of the images are of high quality, ensuring that each visual element, from the vibrant Betta fish to the subtle water textures, is crisply rendered. The color accuracy is satisfactory, yet I believe there's scope to experiment more boldly with the palette to better capture the vibrancy of the Betta fish. While I followed an established grid and format, achieving a functional layout, I see opportunities to further explore layout variations for improved aesthetic balance and user engagement. The typography, particularly after changing the title font as suggested, is more readable, enhancing user engagement. My efforts in converting the original files for screen publication were thorough, but this process highlighted areas where my technical skills could be enhanced. Overall, the project reflects a strong foundation in design, but also underscores the continuous journey of learning and improvement in my craft.
