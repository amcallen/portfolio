COM 232 - Desktop Publishing

TOWN Mission Statement and Brief:

To bring quality, style and the wish for good fortune to all of our guests. We provide a high-end experience through Chinese cuisine. The client intends to open a restaurant and is looking to develop a variety of materials in order to advertise. You will be tasked with creating a table tent, menu design, and brochure, utilizing elements and specifications in the Brand Style Guide the client has provided.

TOWN Vision

TOWN combines a variety of chinese cuisine to excite and delight our customers. Our vision for the future is to create experiential dining that is more than just a night out. We aim to bring quality and luxury across all aspects of our brand. The approach of TOWN is to develop our brand with the understanding of both our culture and consumer insights. Within our vision always lives the promise of inspiring creativity, conversation and quality. Our audience is a high-end clientele who values a dining experience. The age range of our customers are from early 30s-60s. We would like them to come back for both personal dining and events.

Table Tent

To create the final design of the table tent, I had to think about how I wanted the project to look like as a whole. Once I had the idea in mind, I set to work in creating shapes that will be consistent throughout the designs. I also wanted to choose the right font to be the main font of choice. I went with LEMON MILK. I loved how the font look and I especially loved that it was a sans-serif font which I find, is easier to read. I played around with the orientation of the triangles until I was happy with its final locations. I decided to do a wine menu with the back portion of the table tent because I thought about the target audience for the brand, and I know that people of the upper-class do love their expensive wine.

Brochure

I wanted to keep the same styles of my other designs to continue to keep all 3 cohesive. I took my table tent design and incorporated it to the exterior side of the brochure. I used the same font (LEMON MILK) from both the table tent and the menu design in this brochure. I also used Times New Roman so that the LEMON MILK font wouldn’t look so dominating. I chose to put the vision and mission statement on the brochure because I think it is important that the client’s message would be seen by many people and hopefully entice new customers to enjoy their restaurant. For the interior of the brochure, I used my menu design to tie it all together. I included a short menu to give potential customers an idea of what the client serves. I think my brochure design has fully encompassed what the client wanted by keeping it classy and clean.

Menu

In order to create this menu, I thought it was important for me to understand the direction I was heading with my table tent first. I want everything to have a cohesive feel, so I made sure I was very happy with my revisions first. After I figured out my final design for my table tent, I thought that it would be neat to continue the style through to the menu. I chose the colors the client provided in their branding presentation so that I could embody the feel completely. The client’s mission is to “create experiential dining that is more than just a night out.” They want to bring quality and luxury throughout their entire brand. With this information I kept my design minimal, modern and sleek. Instead of adding images which I felt might take away from the look I wanted, I decided to add some icons that looked good to represent the different catalogues of food. I also altered the colors of each icon so that it keeps the design lively. I continued the same font from my table tent design because I love the look of this sans-serif font. It is very modern and just has a nice look to it, as well as it is very easy to read. I kept the layout as basic as possible. In my opinion, I think of high-class restaurants as very minimalistic, so that is why I kept all my design decisions as minimal as I could.
