GRA 332 - Digital Publishing

Assignment

The final project for this course is the creation of an interactive magazine publication package. Advances in technology mark a paradigm shift toward electronic reading of traditionally printed publications. As the sale of printed magazines and newspapers continues to decline, organizations are seeking innovative methods to improve customer engagement and brand loyalty. Digital publishing and creating apps offer readers and subscribers additional platforms to reach new audiences while also providing additional revenue sources via a global audience.

Interactive Magazine

Background

I have chosen to convert the SaveOurSeas Digital Magazine Issue #4, to an interactive version for this project. The goal is to create an interactive magazine publication that will improve customer engagement and brand loyalty. This will entail adding features that will engage the user. The target audience for this project would be science and animal enthusiasts. They may be looking to do more research on the projects or want to donate to the efforts being made by the foundation. A great digital publication competitor would be Eco Magazine. They offer a free digital subscription for readers around the globe. This audience would be better suited with systems that are organizational and labeled.

Design Process

In the development phase, substantial time and effort were dedicated to ideation and brainstorming techniques. This process began with the composition of a detailed design brief that set the strategic framework for the project, followed by the creation of numerous thumbnails, which served as preliminary sketches to explore various layout options. This iterative approach allowed for a thorough exploration of creative avenues, ensuring that the final interactive magazine application was both innovative and user-centric. The commitment to creativity and originality is indeed apparent in the final work. Each design choice, from layout to interactivity, was informed by a deep brainstorming process, where multiple ideas were generated and refined to achieve the best possible user experience.

There's also clear evidence of ethical considerations within the project. Throughout the design process, I ensured that all content was used responsibly, with respect for copyright and intellectual property rights. This adherence to ethical guidelines is particularly important in the realm of digital publications, where the ease of content replication heightens the responsibility on designers to act with integrity. In designing the "Save Our Seas" interactive digital magazine, I have carefully applied the elements and principles of design to ensure that the project is visually compelling and functionally superior. I chose lines and shapes that contribute to a clear structure, with horizontal lines and rectangular blocks creating a sense of order and stability. The color scheme, dominated by blues and whites, not only reflects the aquatic theme but also ensures that the text stands out for easy reading.

To convey depth within the digital space, I integrated images and videos, giving life to the whales and the boxed water product, which also contributes to the visual narrative of the publication. I have used variations in value to create a hierarchy that naturally draws the reader's eye to headlines and essential information, while simulated textures in the imagery add depth and interest. High contrast is employed to emphasize key areas of the layout, ensuring legibility and focus. I've balanced the layout with ample white space to prevent crowding and promote an organized reading experience. Movement and rhythm are also core to my design approach, guiding the reader through the content in a seamless flow. I've been mindful of proportion and hierarchy, using size variations to help readers navigate the content intuitively. Repetition in design elements like the water droplet motif in the logo contributes to the rhythm and cohesiveness of the publication's look and feel. To maintain unity throughout the magazine, I've ensured consistency in design choices, while introducing variety with different interactive elements and imagery to keep the reader engaged and interested.
