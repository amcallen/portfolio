GRA 101 - B a s i c D e s i g n a n d C o l o r T h e o r y

Assignment

Develop a multilayered collage in which you incorporate typography and digital images (preferably images you have taken yourself) into a final piece that reflects your own style and voice while showing your grasp of the basic elements and principles of graphic design. First, select a name for a fictitious graphic design business that you are starting. Be creative with the selection of your business name as this is the possible first step for your career and something you can use as a portfolio piece to market your work and get jobs as a graphic designer. Using this business name as the major component of the design, choose fonts and textural images to create a collage design, incorporating various elements and principles of design including color, typography, balance, and hierarchy. Photoshop masking techniques, layering, transform features, and effects will all be used to create a composition that captures the visual message you feel your business name conveys.

Background

The business name I chose to represent my fictitious graphic design business is Tertiary. According to the Merriam Webster dictionary, tertiary means “of third rank, importance, or value.” On the color spectrum, tertiary colors are the outcome of mixing primary and secondary colors. Taking these two definitions, I really liked the idea of my business being defined as important and valuable but also still having that connection to art. My art style changes from project to project, I like to adapt to my clients and their needs. I also am a very free-thinking person; I don’t like to look at things in just one way. Working with a name like “Tertiary” will keep the doors open for creativity because its meaning is not restrictive and allows for the mixing of ideas constantly so there won’t be a need to rebrand.

Design Process

To begin, I made a file in Photoshop that is 11"h x17"w horizontal layout (300 ppi CMYK). I then pulled out a sketchbook to start thinking about the message and how I’m going to create a design that would showcase that. I also gave thought on how I would incorporate some design elements. Once I decided I wanted a modern approach to this collage, I began looking for images that would work with my concept. From there, I isolated an image of a hand holding an apple pencil-which is a modern art tool. To emphasize my message, I decided to work in mostly grayscale but leaving a drop of color to keep my message about the importance of color very clear. In the background, I created a shape that looks like it is a piece of the color wheel because most often tertiary colors are explained by looking at a color wheel.
